ANZ Analytics¶

This analysis is performed as part of Task 2 of ANZ Analytics Vitual Experience program. In Task 1 , I had created a visualization analysing the Transaction volume of selective customers of ANZ retail bank.
In Task 2 , Objective is to :

  1. Understand the correlation between the salary and other customer attributes.i.e. how Customer salary is correlated with Customer age, gender , and spending behavior.
  2. I will be developing a Regression model to predict the customer salary based on the income and check the accuracy of the model
  3. As a part of challenge provided , I have to also build a decision tree model to predict salary and calculate the accuracy of model.

A. Prepare Customer Data¶

1. Calculate Customer Annual Salary¶

The source dataset we have contains 100 unique customers with one unique account each. There are around 12k transactions occurred in 3 months.

For calculating the annual salary , we will consider only the Credit transactions of Transaction type 'PAY/SALARY'. Note that all customers are uniquely paid. Some are paid weekly , biweekly and some monthly.

Hence, I will identify the frequency of pay for each customer and then calculate their approximate annual salary from the data.

C . Develop a Regression Model to predict Customer annual salary¶

A simple linear Regression model is a statistical method that helps to understand relationship between 2 variables.The 2 variables will be identified as :

  • Predictor Variable (Y) : This is independant variable. This is generally a feature that describes the dependant variable
  • Response variable (X): This is a dependant variable which we are trying to predict using predictor.
The output of Linear Regression model is a linear function that predicts the response of dependant variable as a function of the predictor variable

In given scenario, we will develop a model that we will help predict the annual salary of new customers. Hence X is annual salary and Y is the features annual_spending (Selected annual_spending over age as there is better correlation value)

The function can be written as :
Yhat= a+bX

Here a is intercepti.e value of Y when X is 0 . And , b is slope of the regression line. i.e the value with which Y changes when X is increased by one unit.
